## Business Structure (LLC, Sole Proprietor) {#business-structure}

### Do you NEED an LLC?

**Short answer: No**, but it's recommended for liability protection.

### Options for Shopify App Developers:

#### 1. **Sole Proprietorship** (Easiest to start)
**Pros:**
- No registration required (use your personal info)
- Simplest tax filing (Schedule C on personal taxes)
- Lowest cost ($0 to start)
- Good for testing the market

**Cons:**
- **No liability protection** (personal assets at risk)
- Less professional appearance
- Harder to get business bank account
- Can't take on investors easily

**Best for:** Side projects, testing the market, first-time app developers

#### 2. **LLC (Limited Liability Company)** (Recommended)
**Pros:**
- **Personal liability protection** (your house, car, savings protected)
- Professional image (builds trust with merchants)
- Tax flexibility (can choose S-Corp election later)
- Easier to sell business later
- Can get business bank account and credit cards

**Cons:**
- Costs $50-$500 to register (varies by state)
- Annual fees ($0-$800/year depending on state)
- More paperwork (annual reports, separate taxes)
- Takes 1-4 weeks to set up

**Best for:** Serious app developers, anyone planning to grow

**Cheapest states for LLC:**
- Wyoming: $50/year
- New Mexico: $50 registration
- Delaware: $90/year (popular for tech)
- Nevada: No state income tax

**How to set up LLC:**
1. Choose a state (usually your home state or WY/DE/NV)
2. File Articles of Organization ($50-$500)
3. Get EIN from IRS (free, online, 5 minutes)
4. Open business bank account
5. Update Shopify Partners account with LLC info

**Services to help:**
- Northwest Registered Agent: $39 + state fees
- Incfile: $0 + state fees (upsells other services)
- ZenBusiness: $49 + state fees
- LegalZoom: $79 + state fees

#### 3. **Corporation (C-Corp or S-Corp)** (For serious businesses)
**Not recommended** for most app developers unless:
- Raising venture capital
- Have multiple shareholders
- Planning significant growth (100+ merchants)

### Tax Implications

**Sole Proprietor:**
- Pay self-employment tax (15.3%) + income tax
- Report income on Schedule C (personal taxes)
- Quarterly estimated taxes required

**LLC (default pass-through):**
- Same as sole proprietor tax-wise
- But liability protection
- Can elect S-Corp status to save on taxes (if profitable)

**LLC with S-Corp election:**
- Pay yourself reasonable salary (subject to payroll taxes)
- Take remaining profits as distributions (no self-employment tax)
- **Saves ~$3,000-10,000/year** if making $50k+ profit
- Requires payroll service (~$40/month)

### Recommendation for Bike Builder App:

**Starting out (Year 1):**
- Start as **Sole Proprietor** to test the market
- Keep it simple, focus on getting merchants
- Costs: $0

**Once profitable ($1,000+/month revenue):**
- Form an **LLC** for liability protection
- Delaware or Wyoming if you want privacy
- Your home state if you want simplicity
- Costs: $50-500 initial, $0-800/year ongoing

**If making $50k+/year profit:**
- Consider **S-Corp election** for tax savings
- Hire accountant (~$1,500/year)
- Use payroll service like Gusto (~$40/month)

## Pricing Strategies {#pricing-strategies}

### What to Charge for Bike Builder App

Shopify app pricing depends on:
- Value provided to merchants
- Competition
- Target market (small shops vs. enterprises)
- Development/support costs

### Pricing Models for Shopify Apps

#### 1. **Free (with paid features)**
**Example:** Free for 1 bike build category, $9.99/mo for unlimited
**Pros:** High adoption rate, easy to try
**Cons:** Hard to monetize, freeloaders

#### 2. **Freemium with trial**
**Example:** 7-day free trial, then $19.99/mo
**Pros:** Balances adoption and revenue
**Cons:** Some churn after trial

#### 3. **Tiered pricing** (Recommended)
**Example:**
- **Starter:** $14.99/mo - Up to 50 products, 5 categories
- **Professional:** $29.99/mo - Up to 200 products, all categories, custom branding
- **Plus:** $49.99/mo - Unlimited products, priority support, wizard mode

**Pros:** Appeals to different merchant sizes, maximizes revenue
**Cons:** More complex to implement

#### 4. **Usage-based pricing**
**Example:** $0.10 per completed bike build
**Pros:** Fair pricing, aligns incentives
**Cons:** Unpredictable revenue, hard for merchants to budget

#### 5. **One-time purchase**
**Example:** $299 one-time
**Pros:** Big upfront revenue
**Cons:** No recurring revenue, high price resistance

### Recommended Pricing for Bike Builder

### Pricing Strategy Tips

1. **Start higher, offer discounts**
   - Launch at $29.99/mo
   - Offer $19.99/mo early bird pricing
   - Grandfather early customers at lower price

2. **Offer annual plan discount**
   - Monthly: $29.99
   - Annual: $299.99 ($25/mo, save 17%)
   - Gets committed merchants, improves cash flow

3. **Add free trial**
   - 7-day or 14-day free trial
   - Increases conversions
   - Shopify handles billing automatically

4. **Consider usage limits**
   - Free: 1 category
   - Basic: 5 categories
   - Pro: All categories
   - Creates natural upgrade path

## Common Rejection Reasons {#common-rejections}

### 1. GDPR Webhooks Not Working
**Issue:** Webhooks return errors or don't respond in 5 seconds
**Fix:**
- Test webhooks with `shopify app webhook trigger`
- Ensure they return 200 OK
- Verify URLs in Partners Dashboard are correct

### 2. Privacy Policy Issues
**Issue:** Missing required sections or vague language
**Fix:**
- Use the template in docs/PRIVACY_POLICY.md
- Clearly state what data you collect
- Explain data retention and deletion
- Mention GDPR compliance explicitly

### 3. Console Errors
**Issue:** JavaScript errors in browser console
**Fix:**
- Open Chrome DevTools ‚Üí Console
- Test entire flow (install ‚Üí configure ‚Üí use)
- Fix all errors and warnings

### 4. Mobile Experience Poor
**Issue:** App doesn't work well on mobile devices
**Fix:**
- Test on real phone (iPhone and Android)
- Check theme editor on mobile
- Ensure bike builder is responsive
- Test accordion on small screens

### 5. Performance Issues
**Issue:** App loads slowly or causes store slowdown
**Fix:**
- Optimize images (compress to <200KB)
- Use accordion to reduce DOM size
- Implement lazy loading
- Test Lighthouse score

### 6. Unclear Instructions
**Issue:** Merchants don't understand how to set up app
**Fix:**
- Add setup guide in app admin
- Include tooltips and help text
- Create video tutorial
- Write clear FAQ

### 7. Broken Links
**Issue:** Privacy policy or terms URL is broken
**Fix:**
- Test all URLs before submitting
- Ensure HTTPS is enabled
- Verify URLs are publicly accessible
- Don't require authentication to view
